01

Compare the two routes

One week of surf camp in Canggu is fixed. The choice is the second week. Both routes are a chain of short hops, the sights sit on the way. Emerging middle ground: avoid basing in the touristy spots, but still day-trip the popular ones.

Route 1

The quiet Bali

Munduk · Sidemen · Amed · Bukit
Off the beaten path. Nature, volcano, snorkelling, far fewer crowds.
Pros
  • Far fewer tourists, especially Munduk, Sidemen, Amed
  • Waterfalls, twin lakes, rice terraces, Mount Agung
  • Amed for snorkelling and shipwrecks (a friend's tip)
  • Scenic drives, sights all along the way
Cons
  • Covers more of the island overall
  • Little nightlife or social scene
  • No manta rays (but reefs and turtles in Amed)
Best for
Authentic, calmer side, water without the crowds, a scenic road trip.
Route 2
Heads up: this is the well-known tourist route. August is peak season, the famous spots get crowded by late morning. Iconic places everyone knows, not the quiet Bali.

The classic highlights

Ubud · Nusa Penida · Uluwatu
The icons. Ubud, manta rays, Uluwatu.
Pros
  • Iconic sights: Tegallalang, Uluwatu, Kelingking
  • Manta ray snorkelling at Nusa Penida (~90–95%)
  • Ayung jungle rafting
  • Livelier: beach clubs, nightlife, scene
  • Compact, less driving, Grab works in the south

03

Good to know (both routes)

Getting around +
  • Scooters and local drivers are available everywhere, so transport is never a blocker.
  • South and Ubud town: Gojek and Grab (scooter or car) for short hops, no licence needed.
  • Patchy for apps: Munduk, Sidemen, Amed and some Ubud spots like Tegallalang ban app pickups. Use a local driver or rent a scooter.
  • Long legs and day trips: a car with driver for the day is easiest.
Visa & fees (UK & DE) +
  • e-VOA 500,000 IDR (~£25 / ~30 €), 30 days, extendable once. Apply at evisa.imigrasi.go.id, 48 h before.
  • Passport valid 6+ months, 2 blank pages, onward ticket.
  • Bali tourist levy 150,000 IDR (~£7 / ~10 €) via the Love Bali portal.
  • UK travellers: bring a travel adapter (type G plugs do not fit type C/F sockets).
When to go early +
  • August is peak season, the busiest month, very high crowds in the south and Ubud.
  • Tegallalang, Uluwatu temple and the Monkey Forest get extremely crowded by late morning. Arrive 7–8am.
  • Quieter by nature: Munduk, Sidemen, Amed and the north stay calm even in peak season.
Book ahead & checklist +

August sells out. Book stays early on free cancellation, so good places are held but nothing is locked. Only beds are time-sensitive, you decide what to do day-to-day on the ground.

Safety, scams & temples +
  • Temple sarongs: most temples include or lend a sarong with the ticket, or rent one cheaply inside. People outside saying you must buy one to enter are touts, you don't.

  • Very harsh drug laws. Be careful with cheap local spirits (methanol).

Gut zu wissen (beide Routen)

Fortbewegung +
  • Roller und lokale Fahrer gibt es überall, Transport ist nie das Problem.
  • Süden und Ubud-Ort: Gojek und Grab (Roller oder Auto) für kurze Wege, kein Führerschein nötig.
  • App-schwach: Munduk, Sidemen, Amed und manche Ubud-Spots wie Tegallalang verbieten App-Abholung. Lokaler Fahrer oder Roller mieten.
  • Lange Etappen und Tagesausflüge: Auto mit Fahrer für den Tag ist am einfachsten.
Visum & Gebühren (UK & DE) +
  • e-VOA 500.000 IDR (~30 € / ~£25), 30 Tage, einmal verlängerbar. evisa.imigrasi.go.id, 48 h vorher.
  • Pass 6 Monate gültig, 2 freie Seiten, Rückflugticket.
  • Bali-Tourismusabgabe 150.000 IDR (~10 €) über "Love Bali".
  • UK-Reisende brauchen einen Adapter (Type-G-Stecker passen nicht), du als Deutscher nicht.
Wann früh hin +
  • August ist Hochsaison, der vollste Monat, sehr hoher Andrang im Süden und in Ubud.
  • Tegallalang, Uluwatu-Tempel und Monkey Forest werden am späten Vormittag extrem voll. Ankunft 7–8 Uhr.
  • Von Natur aus ruhig: Munduk, Sidemen, Amed und der Norden bleiben auch in der Hochsaison entspannt.

August ist ausgebucht. Unterkünfte früh mit kostenloser Stornierung buchen, dann sind gute Plätze geblockt, aber nichts ist fix. Nur die Betten sind zeitkritisch, das Tagesprogramm entscheidet ihr vor Ort.
